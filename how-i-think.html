More New Titles Needed

During some of my most formative years, I attended a high school that was part of the New Tech Network (NTN). This network of schools focussed on project based learning (PBL). Instead of tests, we had projects. The school system helped “hold our hands” on learning to solve problems. Every problem we were assigned started with us defining a problem statement. The format we used defined who was working on the project, what the action goal was, and what the end state goal was. Every project started with “How do we as _____ Do _____ So that ____”. Immediately following this, we would take inventory of what we already had to solve the problem. We used the “Know” and “Need to Know” format. This helped organize our thoughts as we geared up to start the project. Once we’ve laid out the “Knows” and “Need to Knows”, we drafted our next steps. This would help create a bridge between where we were with what we knew, and where we wanted to be with what we needed to know.

While I don’t formally do these steps everytime I’m faced with a problem or project, I run through these same steps for almost every project I work on. Being able to clearly define the problem, identify available resources, and identify questions makes every project easier. Even the most complex of problems and projects become clearer through this process.
